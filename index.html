The Author

Four decades inside the industrial inferno.

From the Field

Three Rules.
Three Turning Points.

Rule #10 The Law of Relations

The Day the Line Stood Still

A top operator was reassigned based purely on spreadsheet data — no human consultation. A silent revolt followed: production dropped 30% overnight. Senior management couldn't understand why their "data-driven" decision had failed.

→

Applying Buffalo Logic — visiting the worker's home, restoring dignity — dissolved the human bottleneck. Full production stability returned within days.

Rule #16 The Plastic Brain

The Mystery of Line 4

Skyrocketing scrap rates and continuous line stoppages on a precision line. Senior management was certain the cause was operator negligence. Teams had been pressured, audited, and retrained — nothing changed.

→

Microscopic stroboscope verification revealed a systemic chip-breaker engineering defect. The culture shifted from instinctive blame to rigorous experimentation — permanently.

Rule #11 The Law of Simplicity

Beating Robots with Gravity

A plant facing pressure to invest millions in robotics to solve a material transport bottleneck. Capital constraints threatened to stall operational progress. The team felt trapped between technology and budget.

→

Simple gravity chutes and low-cost Karakuri automation delivered identical throughput — at near-zero investment. ECRS thinking solved in weeks what robotics would have taken 18 months to deploy.

The 25 Rules

Excellence
Distilled

25
Rule · 04

Listen to the Whisper

Machines and people warn before they fail. Excellence begins not with intervention, but with listening — before the shout becomes a crisis.

Rule · 08

The First 5 Minutes

How a leader starts a shift defines its outcome. The first five minutes set the tone for everything that follows. There are no neutral beginnings.

Rule · 10

The Law of Relations

You cannot manage people like spreadsheet rows. The informal social contract between a leader and their team is the true operating system of any plant.

Rule · 11

The Law of Simplicity

Before you automate, eliminate. Before you invest, question. The most powerful solutions are often built from gravity, geometry, and common sense.

Rule · 12

The Law of Dignity

You cannot build world-class products with a second-class culture. Dignity is not a soft metric — it is the foundation on which all operational excellence rests.

Rule · 13

The Law of Deviation

Excellence is not about staying within tolerance. It is the relentless pursuit of the bullseye. Tolerance thinking produces average outcomes by design.

Rule · 14

The Vector Law

A thousand improvements in a thousand directions equals zero net progress. Direction matters more than effort. Alignment is the true multiplier of excellence.

Rule · 16

The Plastic Brain

Stay on the right side of the knowledge threshold before drawing conclusions. The most dangerous moment is when you think you know enough.

Rule · 24

Technology Multiplier

Wisdom × Technology = Impact. Zero wisdom multiplied by AI remains zero. Technology amplifies what already exists — it never creates what does not.
